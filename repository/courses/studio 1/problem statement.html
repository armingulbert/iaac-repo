Challenge Statement

This document provides an overview on my challenge statement identification process and result in the scope of Studio 1. Additionally it gives an overview on the discovery and research process to understand the validity, depth and aspects of the challenge.

by Armin Gulbert, IAAC MDEF 1st year  •  12 October 2025

The Brief & Context

At the first session of Studio 1 our group received an assignment to define a topics that we individually research to have better understanding how different factors can impact humans and other species’ life. As I was raised by a teacher mother I was always surrounded by teaching and the difficulties of it. As an adult, I started to have more discussions with her on the rising difficulites of the profession of hers and others (ie. friends of her, teachers in my network...etc.)

Fact / Validity Check

The National Education Association ran a research in 2024, discovering the well-being / difficulties teachers face daily. The survey concluded that stress and burnout is the top consering factor, and it is heavily impacted by student/discipline. In 2025, the University of Missouri surveyed teachers if they plan to leave their current job. The research showed due to lack of support, workload and challenging students make them leave their profession. The Education Week magazine introduced the Teacher-Morale-Index to see the state of teaching in the US. Their latest index concluded that on a −100 to +100 scale the current TMI is at −13. The OECD has framework for well-being of teachers.

Formulated Statement

The future will require

Discovery & Research Approach

Considering the context and background of the problem, scope of the statement, requirements of the course and the timing constraints, the following research approach has been defined.

1. Research Design Principles

  • Using a 1st person perspective to validate and discover the problem
  • Combining quantitative and qualitative methods

2. Research methods

Type Method Reasoning
Exploratory Research Review and analyze researches in the subject Collecting patterns and stats; supporting the interview preparation
Qualitative Research 1:1 Interviews; Focus group meeting Receiving direct feedback from teachers on their experience to identify patterns for large sample validation
Quantitative Research Survey for teachers Large pattern / sample validation of insights collected
Ethnographic Research School- and class visits Collecting first-person-view through observations

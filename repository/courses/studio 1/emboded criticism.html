Sprint 1

Embodied Criticism

What makes me crazy? How does that context of interest impact me?
The following video presents the end result of an individual task, through which I examined a topic from a 1st-person-perspective resulting a manifest.

by Armin Gulbert, IAAC MDEF 1st year  •  19 October 2025

Why this topic?

Since the age of 6, music was always an important part of my life. Now as an adult, I spent many hours and effort to find and listen good music. It has an impact on my mood, energy from the moment I wake up, till I go to bed. But what music brought to my life is more than just listening. Without any research I observed patterns of:

  • finding ways of exploring new music
  • find the right music for the right situation
  • create collections of music
  • actively connect to others through music

It made me curious, why do I have this drive to find, select and share music of other?

How did I learn more about it?

Collected digital evidences:

I reviewed decades of chat messages, emails (mostly newsletters), photos and videos. This gave me a log and evidences of behaviours and actions.

Identify elements:

Carefully review evidences, created a structural elements of each situation (ie. tools, methods…etc.), then formulated categories and connections.

Self-reflection:

In the process, I recalled / observed feelings, thoughts. I looked up global researches and cross-validated my findings with their results and patterns.

What did I learn?

Method to connect:

Situation observations and self-reflection confirmed that the emotions, their format and dynamics, I have along the process, are highly related to bonding and caring. That aligns with findings of global researches.

Heavily emotional:

Along the process, I could observe different emotional states and dynamics. From rewarding happiness and proudness, to anxiety and offendedness. This tells me, in this process I open up and trust others.

It’s methodical:

It is not a random pattern, based on the reviewed interactions. My behaviour has a system of steps, devices and methods. There’s a purpose, set of criteria and mindset of consideration.

Rewarding:

The previously mentioned emotions built a mechanism making findings and sharing rewarding. These emotions make my behaviour patterns worth repetitions.

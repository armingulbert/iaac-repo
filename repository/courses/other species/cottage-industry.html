Cottage Industry

This document provides an overview on the idea and initial concept of a micro-industry. The material describes the received brief, idea trigger and an initial version of the system-level concept and its principles.

by Armin Gulbert, IAAC MDEF 1st year  •  14 October 2025

The Brief & Context

At the first day of the Other Species course we received a task to craft an idea and a detailed system-level concept for a successful cottage industry. Cottage- or micro-industry means small-scale agricultural activities with commercial purposes. The idea is required to be translated into a system-level concept covering the following elements:

  • A poster-format presentation
  • System mechanism chart
  • Detailed procedures, material usage
  • Defined location, bio-region
  • Communication campaign

The Idea

I love cooking. I appreciate good quality ingredients, products; I believe these are the must-have elements of a good food experience. I carefully select what I put into my basket at grocery store, but I have to admit, finding good quality, local and organic spices and herbs is hard. Considering the traditionally excellent quality of Spanish cuisine, eat-out culture and the respect and living of the Catalan culture / traditions gave the idea:

Let’s build a decentralized local herb
growing, production and
distribution system

What does this mean?

A collective effort to cultivate, grow and sell local herbs in a decentralized approach, in an open, but controlled network. Following traditional techniques, following seasonal effects and methods combined with agroecology mindset and technology enablement.

Principles

The following elements give the pillars of the system:

Decentralized contribution

Members of the bioregion are welcomed to contribute to the system. They can be agro entities, enthusiastic cookers / gardeners, local communities. Contribution to share goods is incentivized but not mandatory. Education, common knowledge and technique sharing are also important enablers of the decentralization.

Local & seasonal herbs

The curation of herb and seed selection is one of the cornerstones of the system. That ensures manifestation of high-standard quality, combined with data-driven mechanisms. Every season has its own selection of seasonal herbs that reflect the local bioregion and culinary habits.

Organic

The products, procedures and materials in this system are organic, including packaging techniques and materials. This pillar also covers the communication and commercial activities as well, putting sustainable growth first instead of out-burning paid one.

Agroecology × Technology

The system relies on a sustainable approach that integrates ecological principles with farming practices to create a resilient food system. It is combined with carefully selected technologies enabling decision-making, quality control, security and traceability.

Sleep-O-Meter

The following page covers a prototype creation of a prosthetics heartbeat and biorhythm meter created for an ideation brief received during our “Living with your ideas” course. The purpose of this prototype is to showcase how we can extend the limitations of the human body.

Sleep like a bird
12 sec micro-nap
Sleep like a bear
Month-long deep sleep

Idea

The received brief highlighted that MDEF students must deliver a prosthetic solution that would help the potential user become something else.

There are two major triggers behind the idea of creating a Sleep-O-Meter that helps the user adjust their biorhythm and heartbeat to influence sleep demand and efficiency.

Animals can show different sleep demand and efficiency – like birds require the least amount of sleep, while certain species take long, even quarter-year-long winter sleep.

The second driver of the idea is a common situation humans face: our sleep demand can change temporarily for short or long periods, even years.

Prototype

The current prototype is a low-fidelity one, and its purpose is to visualise the Sleep-O-Meter’s physical appearance and initiate discussions on potential use and the related user experience. In terms of functionality, the current prototype covers a simple “potmeter-type” experience — testers can rotate the top white wheel replicating an actual meter adjustment. The rest of the elements are not functional; they serve visualisation purposes only.

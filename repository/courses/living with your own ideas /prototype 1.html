Proto #1
3rd-Eye-Perspective
The following page covers a prototype creation of a prosthetics eyewear created for an ideation brief received during our “Living with your ideas” course. The purpose of this prototype is to showcase how we can extend the limitations of the human body.
Idea
Since my childhood I have been heavily influenced by sci-fi movies and games. One of the biggest impacts was Cyberpunk, a Playstation game placed in a utopic universe, where you can create and play with your own character. During the game you can add elements to your body and skeleton that expand your capabilities and over-bridge your human limitations.

The brief highlighted that MDEF students must deliver a prosthetic solution that would help the potential user to be the best of themself. The prototype helps the user to see themself from an external perspective with received feedback from social media channels. The user can get constant feedback on their look, also being able to follow themselves from a “birds-eye” perspective. The AR glasses help the user keep in contact with the real world, while also enabling alternative perspectives using social feeds, Google Maps, NASA live satellite view, and more.
Prototype
The current prototype is a cardboard-based augmented-reality glasses concept that enables the user to stay in contact with the real world, but receive an outer-perspective based on needs and preferences. It encapsulates an early vision of the hardware and interface, helping audiences try and test the solution and trigger critical discussion.
HUD Interface
The heads-up-display design envisions an interface that helps users live daily life, navigate, and solve tasks while providing a third-eye view on demand. Users can zoom into different perspectives based on needs and preferences. The view can include a close outer-POV with integration to social platforms so users can constantly see and feel how they perform compared to their “best version.”
